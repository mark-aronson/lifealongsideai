# Life Alongside AI
## The Bitter Lesson 

### Assignment 
We'll begin by reading Rich Sutton's piece ["The Bitter Lesson"](http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html)

### Discussion Questions
- Sutton frames the "bitter lesson" as a blow to human ego—we want our insights to matter. How should we interpret the value of human understanding if the most effective AI systems don't need it?
- We can only describe our own thinking in relatively simple terms—rules, heuristics, intuitions. But if Sutton is right, the actual machinery underlying effective intelligence resists this kind of compression into human-interpretable concepts.
    - Are we in the same position with respect to our own minds that early AI researchers were with respect to chess or Go—convinced we understand what's happening, but actually holding onto low-dimensional shadows of a much richer process?
    - If so, what does it mean to "know yourself" as a biological being? Is introspection a useful tool or a kind of confabulation we've evolved to live with?

### Redefining Humanity
