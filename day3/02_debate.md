# Life Alongside AI

## Human-machine Relational Debate 

### Purpose 
Now that we have learned about different frameworks and applied them in a novel context, let's dive deeper by debating two that are of particular relevance to our relationship with AI: transhumanism and critical posthumanism. This debate is designed to spark ideas for our final project day tomorrow. 

### Activity 
The debate will focus around these two core questions: 
- Is the desire to transcend human limitations a form of progress or a rejection of what makes us human?
- Should we use technology to transcend human limitations, or should we focus on understanding and navigating our existing human-technology entanglements?

To start, the class will divide into these two sides: 
1. The transhumanists - argue that using machiens to transcend human limitation is a form of pgoress and this is something we should do. 
2. The Critical Posthumanists - argue transcending human limitations is a rejection of what makes us human and we should focus on navingating our existing human-technology entanglements.

The debate for question will proceed as follows: 
- Prep time (10 minutes)
- Opening argument for each side (2 minutes each)
- Rebuttals and further points (15 minutes)
- Pause to prep for closing arguments (5 minutes)
- Closing arguments (1 minute each)

After the assigned sides, we will open up the discussion for you to express personal opinions and views: 
- Take ten mintues after the debate to reflect and journal 
- Open discussion 

### Grading 
During the debate, I will be looking for participation from all team members. I will also be looking for the ability to argue your assigned side. During the open discussion, I am looking for you to agree with or reject the arguments you made during the assigned part.
