# Life Alongside AI

## Final Reflection 

### Purpose
For four days, you've been along the ride the course was designed for - taking us from reflecting on our human experience to understanding biological information processing from the cellular level to artificial neural networks and human-machine relational frameworks, and finally to put it all together into a final project.

The class operated on the premise that, by diving into the details of how these work, we can learn something useful about how we ought to live our lives. You did that work and adopted that argument for your final project. 

Now is your chance to question the premise of the class: do you think that, by diving into these details of how these systems work, we can learn something useful about how to go about our lives? Or are these things sufficiently emergent such that our macroscopic experience is qualitatively removed from the microscopic properties? 

I will be using these reflections to guide any future iteration of the course (if the consensus is the premise worked, I will iterate the design. If the consensus is the premise is rejected, I will redesign the course around questioning the premise from the get-go). 

### Activity 
Reflect on whether you agree or disagree with the premise of the course and write a journal entry arguing your stance. Two notes:
1. I will not be offended if you disagree, questioning this premise is part of the reason I created this course!
2. Do not make this about the quality/organization of the course (please save those notes for the course eval), but rather focus on the premise of the course. 

### Grading
I will be looking for your ability to back your argument. If you agree with the premise, I will be looking for more than just a regurgitation of the course materials - answer WHY this was an effective strategy. If you disagree, where in the course structure did this breakdown?
