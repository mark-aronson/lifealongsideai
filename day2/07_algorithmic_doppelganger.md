# Life Alongside AI

## The Algorithmic Doppelganger

### Opening Scenario (5 minutes)
Imagine an AI system has been trained on every piece of data about you - your purchase history, location data, social media activity, biometric readings, communication patterns, even brain scans. This AI can now predict your choices with 99.9% accuracy: what you'll eat for breakfast, who you'll vote for, when you'll get married, what career moves you'll make, even how you'll respond to this very question.

### Initial prompt
If this algorithmic doppelganger could predict all your future choices perfectly, would it 'be' you in any meaningful sense?

### Guided Discussion Questions

#### Layer 1: Prediction vs. Identity (8-10 minutes)
- "What's the difference between predicting someone's behavior and understanding who they are?"
- "If two systems make identical predictions about you, but one is based on statistical patterns and the other on deep personal knowledge, are they equivalent?"
- "Does it matter how the AI arrives at its predictions, or only that it gets them right?"

#### Layer 2: The Role of Consciousness (8-10 minutes)
- "Your doppelganger can predict you'll choose chocolate over vanilla, but does it want chocolate? Does it experience the pleasure of eating it?"
- "If the AI can predict you'll feel sad watching a certain movie, but doesn't itself feel sad, what does that tell us about the nature of prediction vs. experience?"
- "Could there be aspects of 'you' that exist even if they don't influence your observable behavior?"

#### Layer 3: Change and Growth (8-10 minutes)
- "Humans sometimes surprise themselves - they act out of character, have epiphanies, or change dramatically. How would this affect your doppelganger?"
- "If you learned about your doppelganger's predictions, would that knowledge change your behavior? What happens to the predictions then?"
- "Is the capacity for unpredictable growth essential to being human?"

#### Layer 4: The Paradox of Self-Knowledge (8-10 minutes)
- "You might not be able to predict your own behavior as well as this AI can. Does that mean it knows you better than you know yourself?"
- "What if the AI identifies patterns in your behavior that you're not consciously aware of - like micro-expressions or decision-making biases? Is that 'deeper' knowledge?"
- "If someone else's behavioral predictions of you are more accurate than your own self-predictions, who has the 'truer' model of you?"

### Synthesis Questions (5-7 minutes)
- "What does this thought experiment reveal about the difference between behavioral modeling and genuine understanding?"
- "How does this connect to what we've learned about how neural networks 'learn' from data?"
- "What are the implications for how we think about AI systems that claim to 'understand' human behavior?"
